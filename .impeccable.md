## Design Context

### Users
Crypto degens — people who genuinely believe they can call the bottom, have been wrong repeatedly, and are self-aware enough to find that funny. They use this in private (logging their own shame) and in public (sharing the card on X to flex their conviction). Context: late night, probably watching charts, probably holding bags. Mobile-first is likely but desktop matters for the share card flow.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: dark, dry, aggressive.

The tone is a roast comedian who actually likes you. Not mean-spirited — you're in on the joke. Like a crypto AA meeting where everyone shows up voluntarily and brags about their step count. Think: deadpan sports stats announcing a loss. The humor is in the understatement.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark only. Warm-dark surfaces (#0d0c0c), single red accent (#e03535). No gradients, no blue, no purple. The concept only works in dark mode.
- **Fonts**: Cabinet Grotesk (display, 700–900) + Satoshi (body, 400–700) from Fontshare. Already in use — good choices, keep them.
- **Visual tone**: More editorial / punchy than dashboard. Think a sports newspaper back page, not a SaaS admin panel. Bold numbers, dry copy, unexpected moments of personality.
- **Anti-reference**: Not a dashboard tool. Not Robinhood. Not a meme-coin casino UI. No gradients, no neon, no glassmorphism.
- **Open to**: Pushing the aesthetic further — more editorial character, easter eggs, moments of unexpected fun. The current design is coherent but safe; there's room to make it more memorable.

### Design Principles
1. **The number is the hero.** Every layout decision should serve the big counter. Everything else is context.
2. **Dry beats loud.** Humor comes from understatement and precise copy, not visual noise. A well-placed label lands harder than an animation.
3. **Earn the easter eggs.** Hidden moments of delight reward exploration without cluttering the primary experience. They should feel discovered, not announced.
4. **One accent, maximum.** Red and red only. When everything can be red, nothing is emphatic. When only one thing is red, it owns the room.
5. **Dashboard is the enemy.** If a layout choice could appear in a SaaS analytics tool, rethink it. This is a shame counter for degenerates, not a KPI report.
